Fix Android/Samsung mobile card visibility, especially during bidding.

Issue:
On Galaxy S23 Ultra / Android browser, cards in hand appear too dark during bidding or when they are not currently playable. On iPhone they look fine.

Goal:
Cards should remain readable on all devices, especially during bidding, even when disabled, waiting, or not currently playable.

Core requirements:
1. Do not dim the whole hand on Android.
2. Do not apply opacity, brightness filters, grayscale, dark overlays, blur, or disabled-card styling to card faces just because it is not the player’s turn.
3. Disabled/unplayable cards can be non-clickable, but their rank and suit must stay visible.
4. Use pointer-events: none or game logic to prevent clicking instead of making cards dark.
5. Make sure red suits are clearly red and black suits are clearly dark/black on light card faces.
6. Keep currently playable cards highlighted, but do not make non-playable cards unreadable.
7. Test responsive CSS for Android Chrome/Samsung Internet widths around 360px-430px.

Bidding-specific requirements:
1. When game phase is "bidding", all cards in the player hand should stay fully visible and readable.
2. During bidding, the cards can be locked from being played, but they should not look disabled.
3. Do not apply opacity, brightness filters, grayscale, dark overlays, blur, or disabled-card styling during bidding.
4. Use game logic or pointer-events: none to prevent card clicks during bidding.
5. Once bidding ends, normal playable-card highlighting can resume.
6. This should fix Android/Samsung users who cannot clearly see their cards while deciding their bid.

Likely fix:
Search CSS for disabled, inactive, waiting, not-turn, card-disabled, hand-disabled, bidding, phase-bidding, opacity, filter, brightness, grayscale, or overlay rules affecting cards.

Remove heavy opacity/filter from card text and card faces.

Use a subtle border, glow, or outline for playable cards instead of dimming the rest.

Important:
Bidding requires players to see their full hand clearly, so never hide, dim, blur, or darken cards during the bidding phase.
